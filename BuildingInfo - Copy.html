Basic Building Information

The Robert L. Preger Intelligent Workplace also called IW is a lived-in office, research, and educational environment for the Center for Building Performance and Diagnostics at CMU (Lee, 2020). The building is a rooftop addition to the Margaret Morrison Carnegie Hall, and is designed to be modular, flexible, and open to innovation.

Architect: Bohlin Cywinski Jackson (BCJ)
Client: Center for Building Performance & Diagnostics (CBPD)
Completed: 1997
Area: 7,000 sqft
Total Cost: $4,000,000

Brief History of the Building

The Center for Building Performance & Diagnostics (CBPD) was first established in 1981 (Lee, 2020). In 1988, driven by a desire to research the future of the workplace, the CMU faculty in the CBPD established the Advanced Building Systems Integration Consortium (ABSIC). The consortium also included innovative manufacturing leaders to fund research on the productivity and wellbeing of occupants in the workplace. The planning for the Intelligent Workplace officially began in 1992 after a three-year study of innovative offices worldwide.

In 1992, Swiss architect Pierre Zoelly collaborated with the research staff at the Center for Building Performance and Diagnostics in the design of the IW (“Intelligent Workplace”). The IW was completed in 1997 and used to demonstrate the reformation in building envelope, interior, heating, ventilation, and air conditioning , and telecommunication systems. The systems, components, and materials are continuously updated as research and technology advances. The IW is mainly used as a test bed for evaluating the performance of the latest processes and products. As a result, the IW becomes a long period proof, study, and tutoring project for the Advanced Building Systems Consortium.
